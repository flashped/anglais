Why Cuba?

A Gem in
the Caribbean

Cuba is unlike any other destination on Earth — a living museum where colonial architecture meets revolutionary spirit, where the rhythms of son, salsa, and rumba pour from every corner.

Rooted in indigenous Taíno traditions, shaped by Spanish colonization since 1492, and infused with rich African heritage, Cuba's culture is a breathtaking tapestry of music, dance, art, and cuisine.

From the colorful streets of Havana to the lush valleys of Viñales and the turquoise shores of Varadero — every corner of this island tells a story.

The Journey

Your Week
in Cuba

Day 1–2
Havana, Cuba

Arrival & Discovery
of the Capital

Touch down in Havana and be swept into the heart of the city. Settle into your hotel in the historic center, then step out into La Habana Vieja — a UNESCO World Heritage maze of colorful façades, ancient plazas, and the irresistible pulse of Cuban street life.

On day two, explore the iconic Plaza de la Catedral, wander through vibrant local markets, and end the afternoon in the most Cuban way possible: cruising the city in a vintage American car, windows down, music up.

La Habana Vieja Plaza de la Catedral Vintage Car Tour Local Markets Street Music

Day 3–4
Viñales Valley

Nature &
Cuban Culture

Three hours west of Havana, the dramatic mogote mountains of Viñales rise from lush tobacco fields. Hike through one of Cuba's most breathtaking landscapes in the morning — a scene unchanged for centuries.

Visit a working tobacco plantation and watch farmers hand-roll the famous Cuban cigars, then descend into the Cueva del Indio — an underground river cave explored partly by boat. The day ends with artisan crafts and local encounters in the village.

Mogote Hike Tobacco Plantation Cueva del Indio Boat Ride Local Crafts

Day 5–7
Varadero

Sun, Sea &
Farewell

Cuba's premier beach resort awaits. Varadero stretches along a turquoise peninsula with some of the finest white sand in the Caribbean. Mornings are for action — snorkeling, kayaking, beach volleyball — while afternoons drift lazily along the shore.

A boat excursion takes you out to discover the marine life of the peninsula before your final night. On the last morning, return to Havana for a few final souvenirs and one last taste of the Cuban spirit before your flight home.

Snorkeling Kayaking Boat Excursion Beach Volleyball Marine Wildlife
